Art projects

Art Matters

During my journey in University, studying creative and therapeutic arts, I have had a placement within an organisation that pledges to bring awareness to mental health.
The organisation is dedicated in providing a safe space for individuals that want to engage in creative art making.
Working for this placement has been very enriching and fun, partecipants would usually communicate or discovered points of interest in art and we would try to explore those together.
Creativity fun and wellbeing was the focal point for this project.

The HeARTh Gallery

A university project in partnership with the University Llandough Hospital, concluded with an exhibition in the Hearth Gallery after facilitating art sessions with elderly patients for well-being.
Main key aspects of this projects were: knowledge and awareness for people living with dementia and in healthcare.
The work exhibited had to be appropriate for the setting, following a certain criteria, and approved by stakeholders.
I am very pleased I took part in this project and I was happy to donate my work to the hospital ward, hoping it would bring happiness to anyone watching it.

Part of Ponty art trail

My next big project will be the Art Trail in Pontypridd.
This project is my Graduate Exhibition and my artwork will take place inside Storyville Books on 8 Mill Street.
The Art Trail, besides showcasing artworks and art activities, also contributes to promoting small businesses in Pontypridd.
Feel free to stop by on the following dates: 17th, 18th and 19th of May.
